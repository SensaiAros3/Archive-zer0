Z-008

Object Class: Eclipse ◎

Containment Status: Restricted Display (Mirror Chamber)

Containment Procedures:
Z-008 is housed in a hermetically sealed mirror chamber with controlled lighting.
Observers must be monitored to prevent psychological destabilization.
All interactions require consent forms and daily mental health check-ins.
    
Description:
The Mirror of Forgotten Reflections shows a version of the observer
who has lost all personal memories and identity.
Repeated exposure can gradually erase autobiographical memory,
leaving only a sense of familiarity with others’ lives.
    
Backstory:
Discovered in an abandoned Venetian palace in 2020, the mirror was found in a
hidden study behind a collapsed wall. Artifacts suggested prior owners had left
detailed journals warning of memory loss and identity erosion.

Logs:

Log 1 – Initial Discovery
Date: 2020‑09‑18
Personnel Involved: Archivist L. Moretti
Summary:
Upon viewing, the mirror displayed an unfamiliar version of Moretti.
Subject reported feeling “detached” from own life. Containment established.


Log 2 – Controlled Observation
Date: 2020‑11‑02
Personnel Involved: Dr. T. Nguyen
Procedure: Limited exposure under supervision.
Findings:
Memory loss appears progressive with repeated observation.
Mirror reflects not only the observer but also subtle psychological vulnerabilities.


Log 3 – Incident Report Z-008-IR-04
Date: 2021‑02‑10
Personnel Involved: Archivist Team Delta
Summary:
An unauthorized observer accessed the chamber and experienced severe disorientation.
Mirror exposure temporarily impaired navigation of the facility. Reinforced containment protocols.
